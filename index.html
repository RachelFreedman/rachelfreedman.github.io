Rachel Freedman

I'm a researcher interested in artificial intelligence, machine learning and value alignment.

I graduated from Duke University in 2017 with an interdepartmental BA in "Artificial Intelligence Systems". As an undergraduate, I studied computer science, neuroscience and philosophy at Duke University and the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill through the Robertson Scholars Leadership Program, and at Oxford University as a Registered Visiting Student. My undergraduate thesis was supervised by Dr. Vince Conitzer, and I am continuing to work the the Duke Moral AI research group on incorporating moral values into a kidney allocation algorithm.
